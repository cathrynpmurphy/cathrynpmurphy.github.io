Thermodynamics and Reliability in Stochastic Circuits

Physical bits are subject to thermal noise, which can trigger unintented bit flip errors. In CMOS technology, errors are typically suppressed by increasing the voltage bias across its transistors. However, miniaturization drives circuits into regimes where the voltage cannot be simplt increased and where engineering must account for thermal noise. The physical origins of this thermal noise is the conduction of individual, discrete electrons, which are stochastically triggered by the state of the circuit. Capturing the thermodynamics of the circuit requires that we build up the dynamics of the circuit from this microscopic event, compromising tractability. We deploy tensor networks methods to tractably build up and solve the dynamics and thermodynamics while preserving the fundamental, discrete origins of stochastic noise.

We use tensor networks and a basis expansion to tractably compute the first two eigenvectors with DMRG. The first eigenvector, which has a zero eigenvalue, represents the steady state, which we can use with local detailed balance relationships to compute the dissipation from local detailed balance. The first non-zero eigenvalue captures to the slowest dynamical timescale, corresponding to the bit-flip error time.

Cathryn Murphy, Schuyler Nicholson, Nahuel Freitas, Emanuele Penocchio, Todd Gingrich. arXiv:2603.04658 [cond-mat.stat-mech]
