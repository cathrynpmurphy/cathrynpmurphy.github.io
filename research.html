Thermodynamics and Reliability in Stochastic Circuits

Physical bits are subject to thermal noise, which in CMOS technology can trigger unintended bit-flip errors — particularly in low-voltage regimes. These errors are difficult to characterize because they arise from discrete stochastic electron conduction events. To address this, we deploy tensor networks to tractably compute the eigenvectors of the full discrete master equation while preserving local detailed balance. The zero eigenvector yields the steady-state distribution and the thermodynamics of dissipation, while the first eigenvector gives the timescale associated with bit-flip errors.

Cathryn Murphy, Schuyler Nicholson, Nahuel Freitas, Emanuele Penocchio, Todd Gingrich , arXiv:2603.04658 [cond-mat.stat-mech]
